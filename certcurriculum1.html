Phase 2 — Core Protocols & Routing (Weeks 4–6)
WEEK 4
Routing & WAN Technologies
  • Static vs dynamic routing, RIP, OSPF, BGP basics
  • NAT/PAT, CIDR, route summarization
  • WAN links: MPLS, SD-WAN, fiber, DSL, cable

⏱ 7–9 hrs
WEEK 5
Wireless Networking
  • 802.11 standards: a/b/g/n/ac/ax — speeds and frequencies

  • Wireless site survey concepts
⏱ 7–9 hrs
WEEK 6
Network Services: DNS, DHCP, NTP, SNMP
  • DNS record types: A, AAAA, MX, CNAME, PTR, TXT
  • DHCP process (DORA), scopes, reservations

  • NTP hierarchy and stratum levels
⏱ 7–9 hrs
Phase 3 — Security, Troubleshooting & Exam Prep (Weeks 7–8)
WEEK 7
Network Security & Threats
  • Firewalls, ACLs, IDS vs IPS, DMZ, honeypots

  • VPN types: IPSec, SSL/TLS, split tunnel
  • Zero trust model, network hardening basics
⏱ 7–9 hrs
WEEK 8
Full Review + Practice Exams
  • 2× full-length Dion Training or Messer practice exams
  • Review all flagged/missed questions — no rote, understand WHY
  • Troubleshooting methodology: OSI bottom-up approach
  • Schedule and sit the N10-009 exam
⏱ 8–10 hrs

Study Tips for Network+

  • Subnetting is guaranteed on the exam — drill it until it's automatic. Use SubnettingPractice.com daily.
  • Don't just watch Messer — pause and take notes. Active recall beats passive watching.
  • On practice exams, score 85%+ consistently before booking the real thing.
  • Performance-based questions (drag-and-drop, simulations) are ~15% of the exam. Practice in Packet Tracer.

Phase 1 — Safety First (Weeks 1–2) · 28% of exam
WEEK 1
Electrical Safety & OSHA
  • Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) — OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147 procedures and steps
  • Arc Flash boundaries, PPE categories, NFPA 70E basics
  • Confined space entry — CalOSHA Title 8 Section 5157
  • PPE selection for electrical, mechanical, and chemical hazards
  • Recognizing and reporting unsafe conditions
⏱ 7–8 hrs
WEEK 2
Codes, SCADA Basics & Tools
  • NEC basics for industrial/commercial — NFPA 70 and NFPA 820
  • Basic SCADA: PLCs, RTUs, radios, HMIs, alarm servers
  • DMM categories and correct usage for voltage, amperage, resistance
  • Testing 4-20mA loops, cable/wire toners
  • CMMS documentation basics — service records and daily logs
⏱ 7–8 hrs
Phase 2 — Maintenance, Repair & Installation (Weeks 3–5) · 43% of exam
WEEK 3
Electrical Systems & Schematics
  • Reading electrical schematics, single-line diagrams, loop diagrams, P&IDs
  • Motor Control Centers (MCCs) — components and layout
  • VFDs and soft starters — basic operation and fault identification
  • Low voltage troubleshooting in water/wastewater facilities
  • Fiber optic fundamentals — understand the basics
⏱ 7–8 hrs
WEEK 4
Equipment, Motors & Pumps
  • Motor types, nameplate data, overload protection ratings
  • Maintaining and replacing: communication hubs, switches, controllers
  • Identifying defects in instrumentation and electronic control equipment
  • Assess health and functionality of pumps and blowers
  • Conduit bending and installation basics
⏱ 7–8 hrs
WEEK 5
Installation & Configuration
  • P&ID reading — process and instrumentation diagrams
  • VFD, level sensor, pressure/flow transmitter configuration basics
  • Conduit, wire, relays, pull boxes per NEC
  • Pneumatic control valve basics and control scheme overview
  • UPS, battery chargers, solar/wind generation overview
⏱ 7–8 hrs
Phase 3 — Documentation, Principles & Math (Weeks 6–7) · 29% of exam
WEEK 6
Foundational Principles & Documentation
  • Ohm's Law, basic power and energy — P=VI, E=IR, memorize the triangle
  • Electronic components: diodes, transistors, resistors, capacitors, inductors
  • Ladder logic and function block programs — basic reading only
  • Blueprints, wire diagrams, schematics — basic interpretation
  • Maintaining service records, daily logs, CMMS entries
⏱ 7–8 hrs
WEEK 7
Math Drills — Formula Sheet Provided on Exam!
  • Volume: rectangular (L×W×H×7.48 gal/ft³) and cylindrical tanks (0.785×D²×H)
  • Pressure/head: 1 psi = 2.31 ft of water · 1 psi = 27.7 in w.c.
  • Power: P=VI · solve for current (I=E/R) then power (P=V×I)
  • Work through ALL Grade 1 sample math problems from the handbook
  • Motor overload ratings, flow, velocity, rate/ratio/percent
⏱ 7–8 hrs
Phase 4 — Full Review & Exam (Week 8)
WEEK 8
Gap Analysis Review & Exam Day
  • Complete the CWEA Grade 1 Gap Analysis Tool — target any remaining weak spots
  • Re-read all Grade 1 sample questions and answer keys from the handbook
  • Review the EIT Formula Sheet until all conversions feel natural

  • Bring government-issued photo ID and approved calculator on exam day

Grade 1 Exam Tips — Straight from the Handbook

  • Safety is 28% of the exam — the single heaviest domain. LOTO, arc flash, confined space, and PPE should dominate Weeks 1–2.
  • The EIT Formula Sheet is on screen during the exam. You don't memorize conversions — you just need to use them quickly under time pressure.
  • 100 multiple-choice questions, 3 hours. That's 1.8 min/question — very manageable if you're prepared.
  • No experience required for Grade 1 — your application should be approved faster than higher grades. Submit by May 31st for the Summer window.
  • Approved calculators: TI-30x, TI-36x, Casio FX-115, or Sharp EL series (not EL-W516B or EL-W535B).

Full Certification Roadmap

Sequential order optimized for ASAP completion at 5–10 hrs/week. ~26 weeks total.

Wk 1–3

Network+ — Phase 1

OSI model, IP/subnetting, topologies, cabling, switching & VLANs. Build your networking foundation.

Wk 4–6

Network+ — Phase 2

Routing, WAN, wireless 802.11, DNS/DHCP/NTP. Start Packet Tracer labs alongside readings.

Wk 7–8

Network+ — Security & Final Prep

Network security, full practice exams. Target 85%+ on Dion practice tests before booking.

~Wk 9

🎯 SIT NETWORK+ EXAM (N10-009)

Book at Pearson VUE. $369 covered by employer.

Wk 9–11

CWEA EIT — Phase 1

Electrical theory, safety (NFPA 70E), motors, VFDs, power distribution. Submit CWEA application immediately — approval takes weeks.

Wk 12–14

CWEA EIT — Phase 2

Sensors, 4-20mA loops, PLCs, ladder logic, SCADA, PID control, P&IDs, calibration.

Wk 15–16

CWEA EIT — Phase 3 & Final Review

Water/wastewater-specific instrumentation, telemetry, pump controls, troubleshooting scenarios.

~Wk 17

🎯 SIT CWEA EIT EXAM

Schedule through cert.cwea.org once approved. Contact CWEA at (510) 382-7800.

Wk 17–18

Ignition Core — Phase 1

Install trial, Gateway/architecture overview, Designer interface, components, bindings, expressions. Build first practice HMI.

Wk 19–20

Ignition Core — Phase 2

Tag system, UDTs, historical logging, database connections, Easy Chart & Power Chart.

Wk 21–22

Ignition Core — Phase 3 & Final Project

Alarming pipelines, Python scripting, full mini-project rebuild, complete all IU Core courses.

~Wk 23

🎯 SIT IGNITION CORE EXAM

Purchase $350 exam at inductiveautomation.com. One attempt — only buy when consistently scoring 85%+ on IU quizzes.

Why This Order?

  • Network+ first — most structured, heavy study guide support, good warm-up for a study routine.
  • CWEA EIT second — application approval takes weeks, so submit the application during Network+ Phase 3. Study material overlaps with your day job.
  • Ignition Core last — most hands-on, benefits from the PLC/SCADA knowledge you'll have built in CWEA prep. The free trial is time-limited per session so shorter, focused bursts work best.
